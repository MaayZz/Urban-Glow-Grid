Core Concept

The True Purpose Behind the Project

The power grid
is a shared
responsibility.

Urban Glow-Grid goes beyond the simple message of "save electricity." It makes invisible energy data tangible and immediate — showing citizens that every individual choice ripples across the entire city, affecting hospitals, schools, and neighbours alike.

🌍 Civic Awareness

Users experience first-hand how collective decisions shape urban resilience and power stability.

⚡ Real-Time Consequences

Decisions are immediately reflected in LED states and audio feedback — no abstraction, pure consequence.

🎓 Educational Impact

Designed to be exhibited at schools, fairs, and innovation events to spark lasting behavioural change.

Base Board

Wooden / MDF board with a simplified Paris map engraved or painted, divided into all 20 arrondissements as independent power zones.

🏗️

3D-Printed Landmarks

Eiffel Tower, Arc de Triomphe, Hospitals, and Schools printed in translucent filament for natural LED light diffusion.

🔊

Audio Feedback

Four piezo buzzers at each corner of the board provide haptic and sonic alerts during grid stress or blackout scenarios.

💡

LED Network

Individual LEDs placed under each structure, changing colour in real-time based on current consumption vs. grid capacity.

03 / 08

Hardware Architecture & IoT Mechanisms

🧠

The Brain — ESP32 / Arduino

The central microcontroller manages all district states, reads sensor inputs, controls the LED matrix, and triggers buzzer alerts in real-time.

🎛️

Control Panel — Toggle Switches

A dedicated operator interface with labelled toggle switches lets users activate or deactivate individual districts, simulating real grid management.

🔘

Direct Interaction — Physical Buttons

Buttons mounted directly on the maquette allow interaction with priority buildings — toggle a school's lights to redirect power to a hospital.

📡

IoT Connectivity

Optional Wi-Fi/BLE module on the ESP32 enables future remote monitoring, scenario upload, and data logging via a connected dashboard.

Grid Status Indicators
Green — Balanced

All zones powered. Consumption within safe threshold.

Orange — High Load

Approaching limit. Rebalancing required soon.

Red / Flashing — Critical

Imminent blackout. Buzzers activate. Act now!

04 / 08

Gamification — The Experience

You Are the Grid Manager
1
THE CHALLENGE

Random Scenario Injection

The system triggers environmental scenarios — a cold winter night, a summer heatwave, or a major public event causing a demand surge. Each scenario has unique consumption profiles per district.

2
THE ACTION

Manual Load Distribution

Using toggles and buttons, the user redistributes power — cutting non-essential zones (commercial, decorative lighting) to maintain power for priority services like hospitals and emergency services.

3
THE CONSEQUENCE

Blackout or Balance

Exceed the safety threshold and the buzzers trigger a city-wide alarm. LEDs flash red. The city enters Blackout State — forcing a full restart and reflection on strategy.

Example Scenarios
❄️
Cold Winter Night

All residential zones peak. Heating demand spikes across all arrondissements.

☀️
Summer Heatwave

Air conditioning overloads the southern districts. Risk of cascading failures.

🎆
National Event

Bastille Day surge — tourist zones and transport hubs consume maximum power.

🚨
BLACKOUT STATE

All LEDs cut. Buzzers sound. The experience resets — and the lesson is learned the hard way.

05 / 08

SDG Alignment & Social Impact

SDG 7
Affordable & Clean Energy

Urban Glow-Grid directly educates users on energy consumption patterns, grid stability constraints, and the role of renewable energy in preventing peak-hour overloads. By making these concepts visceral and interactive, it fosters long-term awareness of sustainable energy use.

Visualises real-time grid load vs. capacity
Demonstrates the value of demand-side management
Encourages off-peak consumption habits
Highlights the importance of green energy buffers
SDG 11
Sustainable Cities & Communities

The city-scale simulation models the interdependencies between urban zones — residential, commercial, healthcare, and education — revealing how smart urban planning and citizen participation are key to building resilient, livable, and energy-efficient cities.

Models urban zone interdependencies
Simulates priority-based resource allocation
Promotes citizen participation in urban resilience
Applicable in smart city planning education
