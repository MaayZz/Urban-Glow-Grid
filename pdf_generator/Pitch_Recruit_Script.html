Slide 1 Cover — Hook the Audience ⏱ 0:00 – 0:20 (20 sec)
"Imagine you're standing in front of the Paris skyline at night. Every building lit up. Every district glowing. Now imagine you have the power to decide — which lights stay on, and which ones go out. That choice? That's exactly what our project puts in your hands."

[ pause 1 second — let it land ]

"We are Urban Glow Grid, Team 22. And we are here today because we believe our project needs one more voice — a creative one."
💡

Delivery tip: Speak slowly and look at the audience. The pause after the opening line is intentional — let them feel the concept before you explain it.

Slide 2 The Project — What Is Urban Glow Grid? ⏱ 0:20 – 0:50 (30 sec)
"Urban Glow Grid is an interactive installation that simulates a real city's power grid. Players physically toggle which districts receive energy — and they watch, in real time, how their decisions affect consumption, sustainability, and the city's glow."

"We built a 3D replica of Paris — the Eiffel Tower, La Défense, Notre-Dame — all powered by our system. The simulation runs on a screen, and our LED hardware mirrors every change physically. When you turn off a district, the lights go dark. When you overload the grid, it turns red."

"It is tech. It is data. But above all — it is an experience."
💡

Delivery tip: Point to the slide visuals as you mention each element (Paris, LED panel, energy bar). Make it visual and dynamic — you're describing something you can see.

Slide 3 Why Art Matters — The Missing Piece ⏱ 0:50 – 1:20 (30 sec)
"Right now, our team is full of engineers, data scientists, and tech developers. We can build the system. We can make it work. But we cannot make it beautiful. We cannot make it feel like something."

"This is an installation that will be displayed publicly, presented to investors, and experienced by real people. The moment someone walks up to it — the first thing they see is design. The lighting, the colors, the space. That is not engineering. That is art."

"We need someone who understands how art lives in an urban space. Someone from Interactive Installation Art. Someone like one of you."
💡

Delivery tip: Make eye contact with the audience on "someone like one of you." This is the personal invitation. Be warm, not salesy.

Slide 4 What We Offer — The Role ⏱ 1:20 – 1:45 (25 sec)
"If you join us, you are not a helper. You are our Art Director. Your name on the project. Your vision on the installation."

"You will design the physical maquette, define the aesthetic language of the grid, and shape how the audience experiences every interaction. This is gallery-worthy, real-world interactive art — and it goes directly in your portfolio."

"You bring your artistic instinct. We bring the technology. Together, we build something neither of us could build alone."
💡

Delivery tip: Keep it confident and generous. You are not asking for a favor — you are offering a genuine creative partnership.

Slide 5 Call to Action — Close Strong ⏱ 1:45 – 2:00 (15 sec)
"Urban Glow Grid is about making invisible energy visible. Making something complex feel simple. Making people care about sustainability — through art."

"We are looking for the artist who wants to power the city. If that's you — come talk to us."

[ smile, hold the slide, wait for applause or next team ]
💡

Delivery tip: The last line should be said slowly and clearly — it is your closing image. Don't rush it. End with a smile and openness, not relief.

🎤 Anticipated Q&A — After All Pitches

"What exactly would I do?" Design the physical installation aesthetics, create the visual language, define colors/lighting, and help us make the maquette presentation-ready for public display and investor demos.
"How much time does it take?" Flexible — we adapt to your schedule. We meet weekly and collaborate remotely. The creative direction can happen at your own pace with clear milestones.
"Do I need to know tech?" Absolutely not. We handle all the coding and hardware. We just need your artistic eye and creative direction. No technical background required.
"Is there a language barrier?" We work in English and are very open to adapting. We are an international team and this collaboration is precisely about bridging cultures and disciplines.
