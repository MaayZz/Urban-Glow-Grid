Component Placement & Layout
Top Interface: Control Console & Wood Grid
Bottom Circuitry: Microcontroller & Routing

1. Top Elevation Wood Chassis

The topography is laser-cut from 7 Birch plywood panels representing the elevation contours of Paris, securely structured by 8 wood pillars. 21 translucent building diffusers are nested directly into CNC-milled slot coordinates.

2. Control & Display Console

User interactions are processed via 6 rotary potentiometers (adjusting solar/wind intensity) and 3 arcade action buttons. System state is visualized on a 0.96-inch OLED screen and 3 classic status LEDs.

3. Embedded Controller & Multiplexing

An Arduino Uno processes user inputs and drives 6 addressable WS2812B RGB stripes. To resolve the analog pin limit, an MCP3008 ADC multiplexer chip reads all potentiometers over SPI, wired on a 170-tie breadboard.

4. Power & Audio Alerts

An external 5V 4A power supply adapter delivers clean power, regulated by a latching rocker Power Switch. Dual 8-ohm speakers trigger real-time sound cues to alert users of grid overloads.

Upstream Sourcing Strategy
1. Pearl River Delta Sourcing (Raw Electronics) PRD (Shenzhen)

All chipsets (Arduino MCU, MCP3008 multiplexer), sensors, the OLED display, addressable LED stripes, speakers, and the breadboard are sourced from Shenzhen wholesale hubs to secure lowest-cost component integrations.

2. Yangtze River Delta Sourcing (Chassis & Molding) YRD (Taizhou / Jiashan)

Birch timber panels and pillars are procured from local YRD wood mills, while translucent SLA prints are handled by high-throughput Taizhou bureaus. This nearby industrial integration reduces logistics costs and shipping delays.

3. Solderless Breadboard & Rocker Switch Integration Taobao Distributors

Using a centralized 170-tie breadboard for the analog multiplexer prevents manual wire soldering, allowing easy circuit diagnostic testing. The rocker power switch ensures safe local physical power disconnects.
