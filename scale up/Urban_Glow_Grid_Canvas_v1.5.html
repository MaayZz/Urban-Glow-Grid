Design the Offer Worksheet

Analyzing customer problems, solutions, architecture, and differentiators

Customers & Problems / Needs
  • Problem: Invisible Energy
    Urban energy usage is abstract, invisible, and delayed (monthly bills). Citizens cannot optimize what they cannot see.
  • Need: Gamified Civic Tech
    Public spaces and municipal bodies need highly interactive, gamified tools to translate complex energy distribution grid challenges into intuitive, emotional human experiences.
Solutions and Concepts / Architecture
  • The Hero Product
    An interactive digital twin & hardware layout of Paris at a strict 1:15,000 scale.
  • System Architecture
    A 20-zone physical wood matrix powered by an affordable ESP32 microcontroller, driven by an organic, responsive HSL "breathing light" logic.
Value and Pricing
  • How We Win: Gamified Social Equity
    We compete on visceral engagement and extreme cost-efficiency (sub-25€ DIY building blocks vs. thousands of euros for industrial smart displays).
  • Price Logic
    B2B/B2G subscription-based pricing for custom city layouts, paired with a freemium open-source software model for universities and schools.

Worksheet 5 Risks & Bottleneck
05

Risks, Assumptions & Bottlenecks Worksheet

Exposing strategic constraints, critical assumptions, unknowns, and potential risks

Key Assumptions
  • Tactile Preferences
    Institutional buyers prefer physical, tactile, wooden layouts over plain display interfaces.
  • Simplified Metrics
    Real-time carbon intensity data metrics (RTE/ADEME API models) can be clearly simplified for everyday urban citizens.
Unknowns / Must-Learn (Action Plan)
  • Will users engage long-term or treat it as a short-lived gimmick?
    Action Plan: Deploy a 4-week beta test in a Shanghai museum and track average session duration and repeat visits using hidden software analytics.
  • What is the exact wear-and-tear lifespan of cheap mechanical switches?
    Action Plan: Build an automated mechanical testing rig in the lab to simulate 10,000 presses and record failure rates before mass ordering.
  • What are the exact certification costs and compliance pathways for educational hardware?
    Action Plan: Consult a legal expert specializing in EU CE marking and China CCC certification by Q3 to map out regulatory costs.
Major Risks
  • Supply Chain Disruption
    Fluctuations in components or microchip availability affecting production costs.
  • The Novelty Fatigue
    If the interactive experience feels too static, institutions will cancel their recurring software subscriptions.
