Slide 1 Cover & Introduction

Hello everyone. We are Team 22, and we present Urban Glow Grid, an interactive, physical smart city energy stabilization game designed to gamify the complexities of electrical grid management.

Focus: Branding & Hook Tone: Professional & Confident
Slide 2 Concept & Educational Vision
Ismail ⏱ ~30s 📝 67 words
Visual Cue: Ismail points to the six zones laser-engraved on the plywood board to emphasize energy sector division.

Electricity is invisible, making consumption abstract. Our game materializes this strain on a physical model of Paris. Grounded in research from RTE's EcoWatt alerts and ADEME's load-shifting studies, players must make real-time decisions to prioritize resources and balance grid loads, actively embodying UN Sustainable Development Goals SDG 7 and SDG 11.

Focus: Problem Space & SDGs References: EcoWatt & ADEME
Slide 3 Physical Design — La Maquette
Sofiane ⏱ ~30s 📝 65 words
Visual Cue: Sofiane shows the wood baseboard map, points to the 3D-printed landmarks, and lightly indicates the modular spacing.

For the maquette, we chose to physically separate the layout structure from the electronics, which allows safe and continuous wiring iterations. The plywood base is laser-engraved with the map of Paris split into six energy sectors. Key landmarks are printed in translucent 3D filament to diffuse light from the dynamic NeoPixels underneath.

Focus: Laser Cut & 3D Filament Key Choice: Modular Separation
Slide 4 Hardware & Technical Challenges
Abir ⏱ ~30s 📝 68 words
Visual Cue: Abir gestures to the breadboard circuit beside the maquette, highlighting the screen integration and wiring complexity.

On the hardware side, we are actively debugging technical issues. Adding the OLED screen caused I2C power drops, which we are correcting with filtering. The speaker was damaged during assembly, so we have bypassed it for now. To comfortably drive the expanded LED arrays and multiplexed inputs, we are planning to upgrade from Arduino Uno to Mega.

Focus: Debugging (I2C, Audio) Upgrade: Mega Transition
Slide 5 Gameplay & Sourcing Cost
Mohamed ⏱ ~30s 📝 64 words
Visual Cue: Mohamed turns one of the potentiometers to show a zone lighting up, and gestures to the budget cost table.

Gameplay revolves around concrete actions: six potentiometers adjust zone light intensity, push buttons toggle priority building power, and a start button resets the grid. We optimized costs strictly: our direct components budget totals only 193.61 RMB, as the base Arduino boards and wiring kits were provided by our school.

Focus: Dials & Priority Toggles Budget: 193.61 CNY (RMB)

Abir ⏱ ~10s 📝 22 words

Our immediate next steps will be completing the Mega wiring and calibrating the simulator. Thank you for your time, we are now open for your questions.
