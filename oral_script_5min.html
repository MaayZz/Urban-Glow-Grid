🎙 Final Pitch Script (Round 2)

Urban Glow Grid - 5 Minutes Deep Dive
Total Time 5:00 Minutes
Format Deep Dive Pitch
Speakers 4 Presenters
Slide 1Intro
Abir 0:00 - 0:15
Wait for judges to settle. Smile and make eye contact.

"Good morning again, everyone. We are Team 22. For those joining us in this second round, we are here to present Urban Glow Grid. We are building a tangible, interactive way to master the complexities of city-wide energy management."

Slide 2The Problem
Abir 0:15 - 0:45
Point to the audience to involve them.

"Today, our society faces a critical issue: Energy Blindness. We all consume energy daily, but because electricity is invisible, we take it for granted. People don't realize the massive strain that heating a building or charging an electric car puts on the grid during peak hours.

This invisible consumption leads to wasteful habits, overloaded transformers, and eventually, city-wide blackouts. We desperately need to make abstract kilowatts visible and actionable."

Slide 3Our Solution
Abir 0:45 - 1:25
Gesture broadly to the physical maquette.

"Our solution is right here on the table. Instead of another mobile app that users will ignore, we built a 1 to 15,000 scale interactive model of Paris. It uses a Tangible User Interface.

Users become Chief Engineers. They physically grab the controls to manage power fluctuations across 6 different iconic zones. As they make decisions, the model reacts instantly. We use dynamic, high-brightness LEDs to provide immediate visual feedback on the health of the grid. It makes the abstract entirely physical."

Slide 4Why Play?
Mohamed 1:25 - 1:55

"Why gamify this? Because cognitive psychology tells us that we learn best through active, physical engagement. Reading a dashboard induces guilt; playing a game induces mastery.

By forcing players to physically balance the grid under pressure, we are replacing passive consumption with active learning. Our ultimate goal is to empower citizens. We are training a new generation to instinctively understand how to save the grid."

Slide 5Gameplay
Mohamed 1:55 - 2:40
Simulate an action on the board. Turn a potentiometer.

"Let’s talk about a specific scenario. Imagine it's 7 PM on a cold winter night. Suddenly, the residential zone starts flashing red because everyone turned on their heating. A blackout is imminent.

As the player, you must act fast. You use the potentiometers to lower the energy flow to non-essential commercial lighting. You press the priority buttons to ensure hospitals remain powered. You monitor your overall load on the OLED screen. It’s an inclusive design relying on universal color codes—green for stable, red for danger. It’s stressful, fun, and highly educational."

Slide 6Market
Ismail 2:40 - 3:10

"Who will buy this? We have identified two highly receptive markets. First, the B2B EdTech sector. High schools and universities are desperately seeking tangible tools to integrate into their sustainability curricula.

Second, the B2G Smart City sector. Municipalities can deploy these models in city halls and public exhibitions to actively engage their citizens in eco-friendly behaviors, moving beyond traditional poster campaigns."

Slide 7Financial
Ismail 3:10 - 3:40

"Financially, this architecture is highly optimized. We utilized open-source components like the Arduino platform and WS2812B LEDs, and we 3D-printed our own structures.

Because of clever software multiplexing, we avoided buying expensive hardware drivers. Our complete Bill of Materials strictly totals under 200 RMB. This incredible margin allows us to package high-quality educational kits for institutions while keeping our hardware costs extremely low."

Slide 8Scalability
Sofiane 3:40 - 4:10

"To scale production for these markets, we are moving from breadboards to custom Printed Circuit Boards. This will reduce manual assembly time by 95% and eliminate wiring errors.

Furthermore, our software is completely scalable. While this model is Paris, the underlying C++ logic can easily simulate the grid of New York, Tokyo, or any custom city. Our modular design means we can ship miniature classroom kits, or build giant interactive installations for science museums."

Slide 9Challenges
Sofiane 4:10 - 4:40
Show the bottom of the board or reference the 'spaghetti wiring' photo on the slide.

"Of course, building this working prototype in weeks was a severe engineering battle. To avoid a wiring nightmare, we physically separated the wooden layout from the electronics layer.

We had to manage nearly 90 cables in a saturated space, debugging unstable loose connections from jumper wires. We also faced I2C protocol crashes with the OLED screen, which we solved by carefully balancing the current required to power the LEDs and the displays simultaneously."

Slide 10Why Us?
Abir 4:40 - 4:50

"Why us? Because Team 22 is the perfect blend of creative vision and hardcore engineering execution. We turned a complex idea into a scalable, working product in record time."

Slide 11Conclusion
Abir 4:50 - 5:00
Deliver the final lines with strong conviction.

"Energy is the most important challenge of our century. It shouldn't be invisible. We must interact with it, understand it, and master it. Thank you, we are now ready for your questions."
